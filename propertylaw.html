What is Property Law?

Property Law Definition:


Property law is a branch of law that deals with the rights and regulations surrounding the ownership, use, and transfer of real and personal property. Real property refers to land and everything permanently attached to it, like buildings, while personal property includes movable assets like cars, furniture, and intellectual property. Property law governs various aspects, including ownership rights, leasing, buying and selling, land use regulations, and the resolution of property-related disputes. It is an essential area of law that helps define and protect individuals' and entities' rights regarding the things they own.
